Know where you stand with the key factors that affect your credit


Below are some of the most important factors that lenders use to determine creditworthiness. You’ll get an idea of how you’re currently doing in each of these areas, as well as tips for managing your credit responsibly.


On-time payments

To lenders, your history of on-time payments indicates whether you'll make payments on-time in the future.

Oldest credit line

The age of your oldest credit account shows lenders how much experience you have handling credit.

Credit used

Lenders look for signs of responsible credit usage, and the better you are at living within your means, the better it is for your score.

Recent inquiries

With some exceptions, lenders tend to see too many recent inquiries as a sign of risk, so the fewer the better.

New accounts

To lenders, opening too many new accounts in a short window of time could point to credit problems.

Available credit

Having plenty of available credit (relative to amount owed) indicates to lenders that you manage credit responsibly.

Frequently asked questions

Learn more about CreditWise

  1. Who can get CreditWise?

    The CreditWise app is currently available only for Apple iOS mobile devices version 8 and higher and on the web. Anyone over the age of 18 with a valid social security number that can be matched to a TransUnion credit profile should be able to access it.

  2. Is CreditWise really free?

    Yes. Credit Tracker is 100% free to use and you don’t need to provide a credit card number to sign up or access your score and other features.

  3. How do I access CreditWise?

    To sign up, you can download the free CreditWise app with your Apple iOS device version 8 and higher or click “sign up” above.

  4. If I’m already a Capital One customer, do I need to create new sign in credentials specifically for CreditWise?

    If you already have a current online account for Capital One products or services, you can use those credentials to sign in to the new CreditWise app. If you previously had a Capital One account, you’ll need to enter the username and password associated with that account. If you’re a current Credit Tracker user who accesses it through Online Banking and the Capital One mobile servicing app, your Credit Tracker experience in those channels should become CreditWise after you download the new CreditWise app.

  5. What is the difference between the old tool, Credit Tracker, and CreditWise?

    CreditWise is an updated version of Credit Tracker. It uses a new scoring model and has a new layout, look, and feel. We’ve made it available to everyone, instead of just Capital One customers.

  6. How often is my score updated?

    Your credit profile will be updated once a week instead of just once a month, so you’ll be able to sign in and check for score changes more often.

  7. Where does this score come from?

    Your CreditWise score is calculated using the TransUnion VantageScore® 3.0 scoring model, which uses information in your TransUnion credit profile. This information may include your bill pay history, amount of available credit, number and age of open accounts, and recent credit activity.

  8. Why is my credit score important?

    Lenders use your credit score to quickly summarize your credit history and determine how responsible you are with your credit. If you have a good credit score, it may be easier for you to get approved for loans and credit, and to get lower interest rates and fees. Having good credit can also make it easier for you to rent housing, score a new job or get lower insurance rates.

  9. Will using CreditWise hurt my credit score?

    It won’t. You can use all of CreditWise’s features without hurting your credit score.

  10. Where can I learn more about credit?

    For more information about your credit score or report and ways to improve your creditworthiness, you can visit Capital One Financial Education.

  11. What types of alerts will I receive about my credit activity?

    CreditWise sends notifications to update you on your credit status. You can receive notifications for factors that affect your score, including a new inquiry, recently opened account, delinquent account, improved account or bankrupt account. CreditWise also sends notifications for changes to your public record, address or employment information on file with TransUnion.

  12. What is the difference between the CreditWise score and FICO® ?

    FICO uses a different scoring model than the TransUnion Vantage Score 3.0 scoring model used, which is the one we use for CreditWise. Both of these credit scoring models are based on similar key factors that lenders want to know about before offering you credit.

    These are the key factors that could affect your credit score:

    • Your history of making payments on time
    • How old your credit accounts are
    • How much credit you are using
    • Recent inquiries for credit
    • Recently opened new credit or loan accounts
    • How much credit you have available

  13. Where can I find the full Terms and Conditions?

    Right here. View the complete CreditWise Terms and Conditions now.

  14. How accurate is the Credit Simulator?

    The Credit Simulator calculates possible CreditWise scores using information you provide. This educational feature does not generate an actual score, but rather estimates how future credit behavior could impact your TransUnion VantageScore 3.0 CreditWise score. CreditWise can only evaluate the account information that currently appears on your TransUnion credit report.
